Education & Resources

Understanding Concussions & Brain Health

Evidence-based information to help athletes, coaches, and families make informed decisions about brain health and concussion management.

What Is a Concussion?

A concussion is a type of traumatic brain injury caused by a bump, blow, or jolt to the head that changes how the brain normally works. Understanding the science behind concussions is the first step toward proper treatment.

Learn more →

Recognizing Symptoms

Concussion symptoms can be subtle and may not appear immediately. Key signs include headache, dizziness, confusion, memory problems, and changes in sleep or mood. Early recognition is critical for proper recovery.

Learn more →

Return-to-Play Protocols

Safe return to sport after concussion requires a structured, stepwise approach supervised by a specialist. Premature return increases the risk of prolonged symptoms and repeat injury.

Learn more →

Long-Term Brain Health

Ongoing brain health monitoring is essential for athletes with a history of head impacts. Learn about baseline testing, longitudinal tracking, and strategies for protecting cognitive function over time.
