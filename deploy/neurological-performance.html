Trainable Neurological Functions

How do we protect, measure, and optimize the athlete brain — both in the moment and across a lifetime? These functions can be assessed and trained. Evidence varies by method, and Dr. Patel prioritizes interventions with measurable outcomes.

Vision & Visual Processing

Eye tracking, convergence, peripheral awareness, and visual processing speed — the systems that determine how quickly you read the game. Assessable with structured testing and trainable with targeted oculomotor programs.

Balance & Proprioception

Vestibular function and the body's position sense underpin agility, landing stability, and injury prevention. Vestibular and balance training carries some of the strongest evidence in this field.

Reaction Time & Processing Speed

How fast you perceive, decide, and act. These measures can be baselined, tracked across a season, and addressed with interventions whose effect is verified with data — not promises.

Focus & Concentration

Sustained attention under fatigue and pressure. Clinical assessment distinguishes trainable limits from treatable conditions — like sleep or mood disorders — that often masquerade as "poor focus."

Sleep & Recovery

Sleep is the highest-yield brain performance intervention there is. Assessment of sleep quality and timing, travel and jet-lag management, and treatment of undiagnosed sleep disorders.

Autonomic Regulation

The balance between the body's stress and recovery systems — measurable through heart rate and other markers — shapes readiness to train and compete, and can be trained with structured exercise and regulation techniques.

An Honest, Physician-Led Approach

The consumer "brain training" market is full of promises the science doesn't support. This is different — and deliberately more sober.

1

Clinical Assessment First

Before training anything, a full neurological history and examination identify treatable conditions — sleep disorders, migraine, vestibular deficits — that no brain-training game will fix.

2

Measurable Baselines, Evidence-Chosen Interventions

Every program starts with objective measures and selects interventions by evidence: exercise, sleep optimization, and targeted vision and vestibular training. Notably, physical exercise itself is among the best-evidenced interventions for brain function.

3

Regular Reassessment

If an intervention isn't moving your measures, it gets changed. Progress is judged by objective data against your own baseline — not testimonials or in-app scores. No inflated guarantees of performance gains.

Neurological Performance FAQs

Straight answers — including what the evidence supports and what it doesn't.

Does brain training actually work? ▼
The honest answer: evidence is mixed for commercial brain-training games, which often improve performance on the game itself without transferring to sport or daily life. The strongest evidence supports physical exercise, sleep optimization, and targeted vision and vestibular training. A physician-led plan focuses on those well-evidenced interventions, establishes a measurable baseline, and tracks whether the training is actually changing function.
What does a neurological performance assessment include? ▼
A clinical neurological history and examination, followed by structured assessment of the systems that drive performance: visual processing and oculomotor function, balance and vestibular function, reaction time and processing speed, attention, sleep quality, and autonomic regulation. The result is a measurable baseline profile used to select interventions and track change over time.
Is this only for elite athletes? ▼
No. Programs serve competitive athletes at every level, athletes returning to sport after concussion who want to rebuild measurable function and confidence, and executives or high performers focused on cognitive performance, sleep, and long-term brain health. The principles — assess, intervene with evidence, reassess — apply across all of them.
How is this different from consumer brain-training apps? ▼
Consumer apps sell a one-size-fits-all product and rarely measure real-world outcomes. A physician-led program starts with a clinical assessment by a board-certified neurologist, identifies which systems actually limit your performance, prioritizes interventions with the strongest evidence — exercise, sleep, vision and vestibular training — and reassesses regularly with objective measures. No gimmicks, and no guarantees that overstate what the science supports.
