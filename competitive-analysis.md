## 3. drneilpatelmd.com: strengths vs. gaps

### What you do better than most competitors
- **Modern UX and content quality.** Interactive brain-region explorer, symptom awareness tool, glossary, PubMed research tracker — none of the competitor sites have anything like this. Kutcher's site is a basic Wix build; Williams' is a templated Scorpion site.
- **Long-tail article library.** Six well-targeted articles (recovery timeline, RTP protocol, sideline recognition, CTE vs PCS, second impact, return-to-learn) — better aligned to parent/coach search intent than Williams' blog or Kutcher's sparse education page.
- **Agent/AI-readiness.** llms-friendly .md versions of every page, structured metadata — ahead of everyone in this niche for AI search (ChatGPT/Perplexity citations).
- **Dual positioning** (sports neuro + clinical informatics) is unique — nobody else occupies it.

### Gaps vs. the leaders (prioritized)

**P1 — Single-page architecture is the biggest SEO ceiling.**
Your expertise areas (concussion management, headache/migraine, brain health optimization, sports neurology) are homepage sections (#expertise anchors), not URLs. Williams has ~25 indexed condition/service pages; Kutcher has ~10; you have 1 homepage + 6 articles. Google can't rank an anchor link for "post-concussion syndrome specialist."

**P2 — Authority signals are generic where competitors' are specific.**
Kutcher: "Team Neurologist, US Olympic Team, Sochi/PyeongChang/Beijing/Milan." Williams: "Cedars-Sinai, KTLA, Healthline." You: "0+ Athletes Managed" — and note: your animated stat counters render as literal **"0x Board Certified / 0x Fellowship Trained / 0+ Athletes Managed"** to non-JS crawlers (I saw this in the raw fetch). That's actively harmful in AI search summaries.

**P3 — No "neurological performance" keyword capture.**
You have a "Brain Health Optimization" homepage card, but Williams owns this space with dedicated pages (peak neurological performance, sports vision, balance, reaction time). The keyword space is otherwise held by tech companies, not physicians — a second physician could rank.

**P4 — No location/geo signal.**
Every ranking practitioner has city-targeted titles and addresses (LA, Park City/Ann Arbor, Florida). Your site has no geographic anchor, which removes you from "sports neurologist near me" and "[city] concussion specialist" queries — historically the highest-converting patient queries. (May be intentional given the Neura Health telehealth model — flagging, not prescribing.)

**P5 — No testimonials, no media/press page, no publications list.**
Kutcher links his consensus-statement authorship; Williams lists every TV interview. You have strong credentials but no third-party proof on the page.
